# 4.2 Rounding bounds of integer variables

> Paper §4.2

> *This reduction is purely mechanical; no worked instance is shown.*

If $x_j$ is integer and $\ell_j$ or $u_j$ is fractional, the bound can be replaced by the closest integer *inside* the interval:
$$\ell_j \leftarrow \lceil \ell_j \rceil, \qquad u_j \leftarrow \lfloor u_j \rfloor.$$
The new bound preserves the integer-feasible region exactly. As a side effect, the row infima and suprema (§notation) become tighter, which can in turn enable further bound strengthening on other variables.

This reduction is essentially a definitional clean-up: most downstream MIP machinery assumes bounds on integer variables are themselves integral, and the reduction makes that assumption true.
