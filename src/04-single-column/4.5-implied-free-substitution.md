# 4.5 Substitute implied free variables

> Paper §4.5

## The reduction

Let $\ell_j, u_j$ be the explicit bounds of variable $x_j$, and let $\bar\ell_j, \bar u_j$ be the tightest bounds on $x_j$ that can be deduced from the *other* constraints by single-row bound strengthening (§3.2), without using the explicit bounds of $x_j$ itself. If
$$[\bar\ell_j,\ \bar u_j] \subseteq [\ell_j,\ u_j],$$
then $x_j$ is called an **implied free** variable: its explicit bounds carry no information beyond what the other constraints already enforce. The case $\ell_j = -\infty,\ u_j = \infty$ (a genuinely free variable) is the trivial special case.

If, in addition, there is an equation of the form
$$A_{iS}\,x_S + a_{ij}\,x_j = b_i, \qquad S \subseteq N \setminus \{j\},\ a_{ij} \neq 0,$$
then $x_j$ can be substituted out of the entire model:
$$x_j := \frac{b_i - A_{iS}\,x_S}{a_{ij}}.$$
The equation is consumed; $x_j$ is eliminated from every other row and from the objective. For *integer* $x_j$ the substitution requires $S \subseteq I$ and $a_{ik} / a_{ij} \in \mathbb{Z}$ for all $k \in S$, otherwise the substituted expression is not guaranteed to be integer-valued.

This reduction is sometimes called the **aggregator**.

By bound propagation on the inequality $x_1 - x_2 \leq 0$ together with the explicit upper bound $u_{x_2} = 5$:
$$x_1 \leq x_2 \leq 5,$$
and similarly from $x_1 - x_3 \geq 0$ with $\ell_{x_3} = 0$:
$$x_1 \geq x_3 \geq 0.$$
So $\bar\ell_{x_1} = 0$ and $\bar u_{x_1} = 5$ — exactly matching the explicit bounds $[0, 5]$. Therefore $x_1$ is implied free, and the equation $x_1 + x_2 + x_3 = 6$ provides the substitution.

## What fires

Substitute $x_1 := 6 - x_2 - x_3$ into every other row and the objective.

| Row | Before | After |
|---|---|---|
| $c_1$ | $x_1 - x_2 \leq 0$ | $(6 - x_2 - x_3) - x_2 \leq 0\ \Longrightarrow\ 2 x_2 + x_3 \geq 6$ |
| $c_2$ | $x_1 - x_3 \geq 0$ | $(6 - x_2 - x_3) - x_3 \geq 0\ \Longrightarrow\ x_2 + 2 x_3 \leq 6$ |
| $c_3$ | $x_1 + x_2 + x_3 = 6$ | (consumed by the substitution; removed) |
| obj | $2 x_1 + x_2 + x_3$ | $2(6 - x_2 - x_3) + x_2 + x_3 = -x_2 - x_3 + 12$ |

The model shrinks from 3 variables / 3 constraints to 2 variables / 2 constraints, with a $+12$ objective constant carried forward.

## Notes

- **Fill-in.** Substituting $x_j$ out of the equation rewrites every row in which $x_j$ appeared as a linear combination of $A_{iS}$ and the original row coefficients. If $x_j$ has support in many rows, this can substantially increase the number of non-zero entries in the matrix. Practical implementations therefore guard the substitution with a fill-in budget and a numerical safeguard: if $|a_{ij}|$ is small relative to the largest coefficient in its row or column, the substitution is rejected to avoid magnifying round-off.
- **Numerical stability.** Because the substitution divides by $a_{ij}$, small pivots are dangerous in finite precision. The aggregator is a recurring source of numerical issues in presolve, and is often the first reduction to disable when a model fails to solve cleanly.
- **Integer variables.** The divisibility requirement on the row's coefficients is restrictive enough that the reduction is rarely available for integer $x_j$ in practice; it is overwhelmingly applied to continuous variables.
- **Why "implied free" matters.** Without the implied-free check, substituting $x_j$ would silently throw away the explicit bounds $[\ell_j, u_j]$ — a correct optimization on the reduced model could then return a solution with $x_j$ outside its declared range. The check guarantees the bounds were already enforced elsewhere.
