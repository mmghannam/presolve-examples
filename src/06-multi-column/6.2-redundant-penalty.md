# 6.2 Fix redundant penalty variables

> Paper §6.2

A **penalty variable** $x_p$ in a $\leq$-constraint is a singleton variable with positive cost $c_p > 0$ and a negative coefficient $a_{rp} < 0$ in the constraint — its purpose is to absorb infeasibility in the constraint at a cost. Real-world models often introduce *several* such penalty variables for the same constraint, with different cost-per-unit-violation tradeoffs.

Sort the penalties by their normalized cost $c_p / |a_{rp}|$:
$$\frac{c_1}{|a_{r1}|} \leq \frac{c_2}{|a_{r2}|} \leq \ldots \leq \frac{c_p}{|a_{rp}|}.$$
The cheap penalties are used first; the expensive ones never. If for some prefix $k$ the cheaper $k$ penalties at their *upper bounds* already make the constraint feasible, i.e.
$$\sup\{A_{rV} x_V\} + \sum_{t=1}^{k} a_{rt} u_t + \sum_{t=k+1}^{p} a_{rt} \ell_t \leq b_r,$$
then every penalty $x_{k+1}, \ldots, x_p$ is fixed to its lower bound $\ell_t$ — these expensive penalties are never needed.

The reduction generalizes to integer penalties with identical weights; non-identical integer weights would require divisibility arguments not typically implemented.
