# 5.4 Multi-row bound and coefficient strengthening

> Paper §5.4

Single-row bound and coefficient strengthening (§3.2 / §3.3) work from row activities computed by treating each variable's range as $[\ell_j, u_j]$ independently. The activity bound this produces is, in general, slack — many points in the box $\prod_j [\ell_j, u_j]$ are infeasible because of the *other* constraints.

**Multi-row** strengthening computes activity bounds using a small subset of constraints rather than just the bounds. Concretely, partitioning the support of row $i$ into blocks $S_1, \ldots, S_d$ and computing
$$\ell_{iS_p} \leq A_{iS_p} x_{S_p} \leq u_{iS_p}$$
for each block — possibly via an LP solve over the block's constraints — yields a tighter aggregate bound $\ell_{iS} = \sum_p \ell_{iS_p}$ than the single-row approach. The new aggregate bound feeds into the §3.2 / §3.3 formulas verbatim.

The extreme version is **optimization-based bound tightening (OBBT)**: solve $\min\{x_k : x \in P\}$ and $\max\{x_k : x \in P\}$ over a relaxation $P$ (e.g., the LP relaxation, or a partial LP using a subset of rows) for the variable of interest. OBBT delivers the best possible LP-implied bounds at the cost of two LP solves per variable.
