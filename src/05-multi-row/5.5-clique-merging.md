> Paper §5.5

A **clique** in the conflict graph is a set of (possibly complemented) binary variables no two of which can simultaneously equal 1. Every set-packing constraint $\sum_{j \in S} x_j + \sum_{j \in T} (1 - x_j) \leq 1$ corresponds directly to a clique on $S \cup T$, and the conflict graph aggregates this information across all such constraints in the model.

**Clique merging** combines several set-packing constraints whose underlying cliques can be unified into a single, larger clique. The procedure has two steps:

1. **Extension.** Starting from a set-packing constraint, search the conflict graph for additional binary variables that, together with the constraint's variables, still form a clique. The constraint is extended by those variables.
2. **Subsumption.** Other set-packing constraints whose cliques are now contained in the extended clique are dominated and can be discarded.

Paper Example 7 illustrates the simplest case: the three constraints $x_1 + x_2 \leq 1$, $x_1 + x_3 \leq 1$, $x_2 + x_3 \leq 1$ each describe a 2-clique on $\{x_1, x_2, x_3\}$. The conflict graph contains the triangle $x_1, x_2, x_3$, so any one of the three constraints can be extended to $x_1 + x_2 + x_3 \leq 1$, after which the other two are subsumed.

Merging produces fewer, longer rows that are stronger in the LP relaxation.
