7.5 Exploiting complementary slackness

Paper §7.5

For the LP min{cTx:Ax≥b, x≥0} and its dual max{bTy:ATy≤c, y≥0}, optimality requires complementary slackness: xj⋆​>0⟹cj​−(A⋅j​)Ty⋆=0,yi⋆​>0⟹Ai⋅​x⋆−bi​=0.

For a MIP min{cTx:Ax≥b,ℓ≤x≤u,xj​∈Z for j∈I} with ℓ=0, although there is no general MIP duality, complementary slackness on the continuous part of the model can still produce useful reductions:

  • Apply bound strengthening (§3.2) to the dual constraints (A⋅S​)Ty≤cS​ over S=N∖I to derive bounds ℓˉ≤y≤uˉ.
  • For any row i with ℓˉi​>0, the dual variable is strictly positive in every optimal solution, so by complementary slackness the primal row is tight: Ai⋅​x=bi​. The inequality can be replaced by an equation.
  • For any column k with ck​>sup{(A⋅k​)Ty:ℓˉ≤y≤uˉ}, the dual feasibility constraint is strictly slack at every dual-feasible y, so by complementary slackness xk⋆​=0. The variable is fixed at its lower bound.

These reductions transfer information from the LP relaxation's dual into the primal MIP — variable fixings and tight equations that hold for every optimal solution.
