Paper §6.3

Two columns A⋅j​ and A⋅k​ (with j=k) are parallel if there is a scalar λ=0 with A⋅k​=λA⋅j​. If, additionally, the objective coefficients satisfy ck​=λcj​ and the integrality / bound types are compatible, the two variables can be merged into a single new variable y:=xj​+λxk​. Every constraint sees only the combination aij​(xj​+λxk​)=aij​y, and the objective sees only cj​(xj​+λxk​)=cj​y, so the model can be rewritten in y alone.

The bounds on y depend on λ and the bounds of xj​,xk​: ℓy​={ℓj​+λℓk​ℓj​+λuk​​λ>0λ<0​,uy​={uj​+λuk​uj​+λℓk​​λ>0λ<0.​

Integer variables require extra care: even if the merged y is integer, splitting y⋆ back into integer xj⋆​,xk⋆​ at uncrush time may not be possible unless the image of xj​+λxk​ over the variables' domains is an interval of integers without holes. A sufficient condition is ∣λ(uk​−ℓk​)∣≥1 when one of the variables is integer and the other continuous.
