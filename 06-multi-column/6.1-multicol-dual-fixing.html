6.1 Multi-column dual fixing for single equations

Paper §6.1

Single-column dual fixing (§4.4) only applies to variables that do not appear in any equation. Multi-column dual fixing generalizes the argument to recover a similar conclusion when the variable participates in exactly one equation r, as long as enough other variables in that equation can absorb its movement.

Concretely: let S+​⊆N be the variables s in row r with cs​≥0, ais​≥0 on every ≤-row, and ais​=0 on every other equation. These are the variables that "move freely upward" in the single equation r. Splitting S−​ analogously and letting S−C​ be the continuous variables among them, the condition inf{ArT−​​xT−​​}+sup{ArS−C​​xS−C​​}≥br​ (where T−​=N∖S−C​) certifies that the variables in S+​ can be fixed to their lower bounds without violating row r — any decrease in row r's activity from doing so can be compensated by increasing the continuous variables in S−C​.

The structural conditions are demanding, so the reduction triggers on a small fraction of models. When it does fire, it can fix many variables at once.
