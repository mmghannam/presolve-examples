4.1 Removal of fixed variables

Paper §4.1

A variable xj​ with ℓj​=uj​ is fixed: every feasible point assigns it the same value. Such a variable can be removed from the model by

  • subtracting A⋅j​ℓj​ from the right-hand side b, and
  • folding cj​ℓj​ into the objective constant.

After the substitution xj​ no longer appears in any constraint or in the objective, so its column is deleted. In post-processing, recovering xj​ is trivial — it is fixed.

Fixed variables arise both directly in the input (e.g., a binary variable forced by data to 0 or 1) and as a consequence of other reductions, especially bound strengthening (§3.2) collapsing [ℓj​,uj​] to a singleton.
